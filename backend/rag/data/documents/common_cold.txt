TITLE: Common Cold (Acute Viral Rhinopharyngitis)
SOURCE: WHO Health Topics / CDC
URL: https://www.who.int/health-topics/common-cold

OVERVIEW
The common cold is a viral infection of the upper respiratory tract, primarily affecting the nose and throat. It is one of the most frequent infectious diseases in humans, with adults averaging 2-3 colds per year and children experiencing 6-8 annually.

CAUSES
The common cold is caused by over 200 different viruses, with rhinoviruses being the most common (30-50% of cases). Other causative agents include:
- Coronaviruses (10-15%)
- Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV)
- Parainfluenza viruses
- Adenoviruses
- Enteroviruses

TRANSMISSION
Cold viruses spread through:
1. Direct contact with infected secretions
2. Airborne droplets from coughing or sneezing
3. Touching contaminated surfaces then touching eyes, nose, or mouth
The incubation period is typically 1-3 days.

SYMPTOMS
Common symptoms include:
- Runny or stuffy nose (nasal congestion)
- Sore throat
- Cough
- Sneezing
- Low-grade fever (more common in children)
- Mild headache
- Body aches
- Fatigue and malaise
- Watery eyes

Symptoms usually peak at 2-3 days and resolve within 7-10 days, though some symptoms may persist up to 2 weeks.

COMPLICATIONS
While usually self-limiting, the common cold can lead to:
- Acute otitis media (ear infection)
- Sinusitis
- Exacerbation of asthma
- Lower respiratory tract infections (bronchitis, pneumonia)

TREATMENT
There is no cure for the common cold. Treatment focuses on symptom relief:
- Rest and adequate hydration
- Over-the-counter pain relievers (acetaminophen, ibuprofen)
- Decongestants
- Antihistamines
- Cough suppressants
- Honey for cough (adults and children over 1 year)
- Throat lozenges

Antibiotics are NOT effective against viral infections and should not be used for the common cold.

PREVENTION
- Frequent handwashing with soap and water
- Avoiding touching face with unwashed hands
- Staying away from sick individuals
- Disinfecting frequently touched surfaces
- Maintaining good overall health through nutrition and sleep
- No vaccine is available due to the large number of causative viruses

WHEN TO SEEK MEDICAL CARE
Consult a healthcare provider if:
- Symptoms persist beyond 10 days
- High fever (above 103°F/39.4°C)
- Severe headache or sinus pain
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Symptoms improve then worsen
- Underlying health conditions that increase risk
