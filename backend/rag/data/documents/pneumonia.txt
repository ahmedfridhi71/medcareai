TITLE: Pneumonia
SOURCE: WHO Fact Sheet / CDC
URL: https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/pneumonia

OVERVIEW
Pneumonia is an acute respiratory infection affecting the lungs, where the alveoli (air sacs) fill with pus and fluid, making breathing painful and limiting oxygen intake. It is a leading cause of death worldwide, particularly in children under 5 and adults over 65. Pneumonia accounts for 14% of all deaths in children under 5 years old globally.

TYPES
By causative agent:
- Bacterial pneumonia (most common in adults)
- Viral pneumonia
- Fungal pneumonia (immunocompromised patients)
- Aspiration pneumonia

By acquisition:
- Community-acquired pneumonia (CAP)
- Hospital-acquired pneumonia (HAP)
- Ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP)

CAUSES
Bacterial:
- Streptococcus pneumoniae (most common)
- Haemophilus influenzae
- Staphylococcus aureus
- Mycoplasma pneumoniae (atypical)
- Legionella pneumophila

Viral:
- Influenza viruses
- Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV)
- SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19)
- Adenoviruses
- Rhinoviruses

Fungal:
- Pneumocystis jirovecii
- Histoplasma
- Cryptococcus

RISK FACTORS
- Age (under 2 or over 65 years)
- Chronic diseases (asthma, COPD, heart disease, diabetes)
- Weakened immune system
- Smoking
- Recent respiratory infection
- Hospitalization (especially ICU)
- Difficulty swallowing
- Malnutrition

SYMPTOMS
Common symptoms include:
- Cough (may produce mucus)
- Fever, sweating, chills
- Shortness of breath
- Chest pain (worse with breathing or coughing)
- Fatigue
- Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
- Confusion (especially in older adults)

Severe cases:
- High fever
- Rapid breathing
- Low blood pressure
- Blue-tinged lips or nails (cyanosis)
- Altered mental status

DIAGNOSIS
Physical examination:
- Lung auscultation (crackles, decreased breath sounds)
- Vital signs assessment

Diagnostic tests:
- Chest X-ray (gold standard)
- CT scan (if X-ray inconclusive)
- Blood tests (CBC, inflammatory markers)
- Sputum culture and Gram stain
- Blood cultures
- Pulse oximetry
- Arterial blood gas (severe cases)
- Urinary antigen tests (S. pneumoniae, Legionella)

SEVERITY ASSESSMENT
CURB-65 score:
- Confusion
- Urea >7 mmol/L
- Respiratory rate ≥30/min
- Blood pressure <90/60 mmHg
- Age ≥65 years

Score determines outpatient vs. hospital treatment.

TREATMENT
Bacterial pneumonia:
- Antibiotics (amoxicillin, azithromycin, fluoroquinolones)
- Duration typically 5-7 days
- Severe cases may require IV antibiotics

Viral pneumonia:
- Supportive care
- Antivirals for influenza (oseltamivir)
- COVID-19: specific treatments as indicated

Supportive care:
- Oxygen therapy
- Fluids
- Pain relievers and fever reducers
- Rest

Severe/hospitalized cases:
- IV antibiotics
- Mechanical ventilation if needed
- ICU care for critical patients

COMPLICATIONS
- Bacteremia (bacteria in bloodstream)
- Pleural effusion
- Empyema
- Lung abscess
- Acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS)
- Respiratory failure
- Sepsis
- Death

PREVENTION
Vaccination:
- Pneumococcal vaccines (PCV13, PPSV23)
- Influenza vaccine annually
- COVID-19 vaccines

Lifestyle measures:
- Good hand hygiene
- Not smoking
- Maintaining good health
- Adequate nutrition
- Treating underlying conditions

PROGNOSIS
Most healthy adults recover in 1-3 weeks. Factors affecting outcome:
- Age
- Overall health
- Causative organism
- Severity at presentation
- Timeliness of treatment

WHEN TO SEEK EMERGENCY CARE
- Difficulty breathing
- Persistent high fever (>102°F/39°C)
- Bluish skin color
- Confusion or altered mental status
- Rapid heart rate
- Symptoms not improving with treatment
